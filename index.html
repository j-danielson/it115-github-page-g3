Faven's Top Album

Dawn FM by The Weekend

Dawn FM is the fifth studio album by Canadian singer-songwriter the Weeknd. Dawn FM is a concept album that underscores helplessness as only a mindset. It's a character's journey through the phases of purgatory, and the eventual acceptance of one's state of being. It was released on January 7, 2022, through XO and Republic Records. The album features narration by Jim Carrey, guest vocals from Tyler, the Creator and Lil Wayne, and spoken word appearances from Quincy Jones and Josh Safdie.

List of songs to listen to:
  • Dawn FM
  • Gasoline
  • How do I make you love me?
  • sacrifice

Mik's Top Album

Cycles of Existential Rhyme by Chicano Batman

Cycles of Existential Rhyme by Chicano Batman: "Cycles of Existential Rhyme" is the fourth studio album by Chicano Batman, a Los Angeles-based band known for their blend of classic rock, soul, and Latin rhythms. The album was released in 2014 and features a mixture of upbeat and introspective tracks that explore themes of love, identity, and social justice. The album received positive reviews from critics, who praised its musical diversity and thought-provoking lyrics. With its unique blend of genres and styles, "Cycles of Existential Rhyme" showcases Chicano Batman's signature sound and solidifies their place as one of the most innovative and influential bands in contemporary music.

  • She Lives On My Block
  • A Cool Blessing
  • Bilbao
  • Cycles of Existential Rhyme
  • Magma

Josh's Top Album

The Forever Story by JID

The Forever Story was a highly anticipated project, as it marks JID's first solo output in almost four years, since the critically acclaimed DiCaprio 2 was released in 2018. Our Generation Music wrote: "With a forthcoming album that's been in the works for nearly a half decade, fans could ironically call the wait for JID's next project 'The Forever Story.'" Conceptually, The Forever Story is a sequel to JID's debut album, The Never Story, released in 2017. The title is a direct reference to the 2017 album's title, and the artwork alludes to the 2017 album's artwork. The artwork depicts the rapper "standing in the middle of a crowd containing people from all walks of life", capturing "the different realities Black people face in America."

  • Rayder
  • Sound Sound (feat. 21 Savage & Baby Tate)
  • Can't Punk Me (feat. EARTHGANG)
